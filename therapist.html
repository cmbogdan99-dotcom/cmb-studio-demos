What we can work on together

Therapy isn't one-size-fits-all. My practice focuses on three areas where I've spent over a decade going deep.

🌊

Anxiety & overthinking

The 3am spiral, the rehearsed conversations, the constant scanning for what could go wrong. We'll work on quieting the noise — not by ignoring it, but by changing your relationship with it.

🔥

Burnout & perfectionism

When "good enough" has never felt like enough. We'll untangle where the pressure actually comes from and build a way of working — and resting — you can sustain.

🧭

Life transitions

New role, new city, new chapter — or the quiet realization that the life you built isn't the one you want. Change is disorienting even when it's chosen. You don't have to navigate it alone.

What working together looks like

No couch clichés, no endless "how does that make you feel." Structured, warm, and honest — here's the shape of it.

01

Free 15-minute consult

A short call to see if we're a fit. You tell me what's going on, I tell you honestly whether I'm the right person to help.

02

First sessions: mapping the territory

We get specific about what's happening, when it started, and what "better" would actually look like for you — in your words, not a textbook's.

03

The work: skills + depth

Practical tools you can use this week (CBT, ACT) combined with deeper work on the patterns underneath. Most clients feel movement within 6–8 sessions.

04

Stepping down, not dropping off

As things improve we space sessions out. The goal was never therapy forever — it's you, resourced, not needing me.

Common questions

Do you take insurance?

I'm an out-of-network provider. I provide a monthly superbill you can submit to your insurance — most PPO plans reimburse 50–80% after the deductible. On our consult call I'll walk you through exactly how to check your benefits in one 5-minute phone call.

How long does therapy take?

It depends on what we're working on, and I'll always be honest with you about my read. Most clients working on anxiety or burnout feel real movement within 6–8 sessions and do their deepest work over 4–8 months. You're never locked in — we review progress together every couple of months.

Is online therapy as effective as in-person?

For the work I do — yes, the research is solid on this. About half my practice is telehealth. Some clients prefer the office; some do their best work from their own couch with their dog nearby. We'll find what works for you.

What if I've tried therapy before and it didn't help?

You're in good company — many of my clients are on their second or third therapist. Usually the issue wasn't you; it was fit or approach. That's exactly what the free consult is for: figuring out whether this time will be different before you invest anything.
