Most wedding photography is staged twice — once by the planner, once by the photographer. I only show up for the first one.

My job is to notice. The look between your parents during the toast. The way your hands find each other without deciding to. Nine years in, that's still the only thing I'm actually good at.

How a booking actually goes

Four steps, no sales calls
01

You tell me the date

Email or the form below. I reply within a day, honestly, even if I'm already booked.

Most couples reach out 8–14 months out, but I've taken 6-week bookings when the date lined up.

02

A 20-minute call

Not a pitch — I want to know what actually matters to you two before I quote anything.

If we're not a fit, I'll tell you and point you toward someone who is.

03

The day itself

I arrive early, stay late, and mostly disappear into the background once things start.

Typical coverage runs 8–10 hours. Second shooter available for larger weddings.

04

Gallery in 6 weeks

400–600 edited images, delivered as a private online gallery you can download and share.

Sneak peek of 20–30 favorites goes out within 72 hours.
