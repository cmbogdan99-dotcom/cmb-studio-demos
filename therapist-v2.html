Hi —

If you're reading this, something in your life probably looks fine from the outside. The job's going well, or well enough. People rely on you. You show up.

And underneath that, something's been humming at a volume that doesn't turn off. Maybe it's the 3am replay of a conversation nobody else remembers. Maybe it's the sense that if you stop moving for even a day, something you've built will come apart.

I work with people exactly there — high-functioning, quietly exhausted, and not entirely sure therapy is even the right move.

It usually is. Not because you're broken — you're not — but because the strategies that got you this far (control, overpreparation, never letting anyone see you struggle) have a ceiling. Most of my clients hit it right around the point they reach out to someone like me.

What we'd actually do together: some structured tools you can use the same week (I lean on CBT and ACT, not vague talk-therapy platitudes), and slower work on where the pressure is really coming from. Most people feel real movement in six to eight sessions. Nobody's signing up for therapy forever.
